Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to buy a property in the Philippines?

For Philippine property purchases, you'll typically need valid government-issued IDs, TIN (Tax Identification Number), proof of income or financial capacity, and if married, your spouse's consent. For foreign buyers, additional requirements apply including proof of residency status. Contact us for a detailed checklist tailored to your situation.

Do you offer financing options or bank loan assistance?

Yes! We work with major banks including BDO, BPI, Metrobank, and Pag-IBIG to help you secure the best financing options. We can also assist with in-house financing for select developer properties. Our team will guide you through the loan application process.

How long does the property buying process take?

The timeline varies depending on the transaction type. Cash purchases can close in 2-4 weeks, while bank-financed purchases typically take 1-3 months for loan approval and processing. We guide you through every step to ensure a smooth and efficient process.

Do you help with property title transfers and documentation?

Absolutely! We assist with all documentation including title transfers at the Registry of Deeds, tax declarations at the Assessor's Office, and deed of sale preparation. Our team works with trusted lawyers and notaries to ensure all legal requirements are properly handled.
