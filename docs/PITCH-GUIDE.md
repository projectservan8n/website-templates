# The Problem-Solver Pitch Guide

## Your Core Message

You're not selling websites. You're selling **customers, credibility, and convenience**.

---

## The Problems (What Keeps Business Owners Up at Night)

### 1. "People Can't Find Me"
- Customer searches "best ramen Zamboanga" → your client doesn't appear
- Google prioritizes websites over Facebook pages
- Competitors with websites steal customers who were looking for YOUR client

**The pain:** Every day, potential customers are searching online and going to competitors instead.

### 2. "I Look Less Legit Than My Competition"
- A Facebook page says "small business"
- A website says "established, professional, trustworthy"
- Customers judge businesses in 3 seconds - a real website builds instant trust

**The pain:** Customers choose competitors who *look* more professional, even if your client's product is better.

### 3. "I Answer the Same Questions 50 Times a Day"
- "Where are you located?"
- "What's your menu/price list?"
- "What time do you open?"
- "Do you deliver?"

**The pain:** Staff wastes hours every week answering questions that a website answers automatically.

### 4. "My Facebook Posts Get Buried"
- Facebook organic reach is down to 2-5%
- Posts disappear after a day
- Algorithm changes can kill visibility overnight

**The pain:** They don't *own* their online presence - Facebook does.

### 5. "I Can't Showcase My Best Work"
- Instagram grid is messy and hard to navigate
- No central place for menu, services, portfolio
- Customers can't easily see what they offer

**The pain:** Their best products/services are hidden in a sea of random posts.

---

## The ROI Math (Make It Real)

### For Restaurants/Cafés:
```
Average customer spend: ₱300-500
Monthly maintenance cost: ₱1,000-1,200

Break-even: Just 2-3 extra customers per month

If website brings 10 extra customers/month:
→ ₱3,000-5,000 extra revenue
→ ROI: 3-5x the maintenance cost
```

### For Service Businesses (Salons, Clinics, etc.):
```
Average service price: ₱500-2,000
Monthly maintenance cost: ₱1,000-1,200

Break-even: Just 1-2 extra bookings per month

If website brings 5 extra bookings/month:
→ ₱2,500-10,000 extra revenue
→ ROI: 2-10x the maintenance cost
```

### The Question to Ask:
> "If your website brought you just 3 extra customers a month, would it be worth ₱1,000?"

The answer is always yes.

- "Menu change? New promo? Just message me. I update it same day."
- DIY sites get abandoned because owners don't have time to maintain them

### 4. Custom, Not Cookie-Cutter
- "I design specifically for YOUR brand, not a generic template"
- Show them the demo - that's proof

### 5. Affordable
- "Agency websites cost ₱50,000+. I offer the same quality at local prices."
- You're the smart middle ground between DIY and expensive agencies

## The Pitch Framework

### Step 1: Connect First
- Be a customer first, or show genuine interest in their business
- Find something specific to compliment (food, vibe, service)

### Step 2: Identify Their Pain
Ask questions like:
- "Do you get a lot of customers from online searches?"
- "How do people usually find you?"
- "Do you get tired of answering the same questions on Messenger?"

### Step 3: Show, Don't Tell
- Lead with the demo: "I made this for you"
- Let the website sell itself
- They can SEE the value immediately

### Step 4: Make ROI Tangible
- "If this brings you just 3 extra customers a month, it pays for itself"
- Use THEIR average prices in the calculation

### Step 5: Remove Risk
- "Check out the demo first, no pressure"
- "We can meet and discuss if you're interested"
- Low-pressure = higher trust

## Objection Handling

### "I already have Facebook/Instagram"
> "That's great for engaging existing customers! But Facebook reach is only 2-5% now - most of your followers don't even see your posts. A website shows up when NEW customers search for businesses like yours on Google. It works alongside your social media, not instead of it."

### "I don't have the budget"
> "I totally understand. Let me ask - how much does one customer spend at your place? ₱300? ₱500? The monthly cost is ₱1,000-1,200, so if the website brings just 2-3 extra customers a month, it's already paid for itself. It's not an expense, it's an investment that brings returns."

### "I can make my own on Wix/Canva"
> "You definitely can! But honestly, most business owners start a Wix site and never finish it because they're too busy running their business. With me, you don't have to learn anything or spend hours figuring it out. I handle everything, and whenever you need updates - menu changes, promos - just message me."

### "Let me think about it"
> "Of course! The demo isn't going anywhere. Take your time to check it out, show it to your team. I'll follow up in a few days, and if you have any questions, just message me anytime."

### "My nephew/friend can do it"
> "That's great if they're reliable! Just make sure they'll be available for updates and maintenance long-term. A lot of business owners come to me after their friend-made website broke and no one could fix it. I'm here for the long haul - this is my business."

## Case Studies: Local Businesses Winning With Websites

Use these real examples when pitching. Both are Zamboanga-based businesses that clients may already know.

---

### Case Study 1: Taza Coffee Co.
**Website:** tazacoffeeco.com

**What they are:** Specialty coffee roaster with 3 locations (2 in Zamboanga, 1 in Manila)

**What their website does:**
- Full e-commerce store (coffee beans, brewing gear, merch)
- Online ordering with free shipping over ₱2,000
- All 3 branch locations with hours and addresses
- "Learn" section teaching customers about coffee
- Newsletter signup offering 10% off first order
- Careers page for hiring

**Why it works:**
> "Taza started as a local coffee shop. Now they ship nationwide. Their website turned them from a Zamboanga café into a national brand. When someone in Manila searches 'specialty coffee Philippines', Taza shows up. That's not possible with just a Facebook page."

**The pitch angle:**
> "Look at Taza Coffee - they're from here in Zamboanga, but their website lets them sell to customers in Manila, Cebu, anywhere. They're not just a café anymore, they're a brand. A website opens doors that social media can't."

---

### Case Study 2: Dennis Coffee Garden
**Website:** denniscoffeegarden.com

**What they are:** Traditional Sulu coffee shop, operating since 1962 (started as Omar's Place in Jolo)

**What their website does:**
- Full menu with prices (hot/cold drinks, Tausug delicacies, rice meals)
- Brand story from 1962 to present
- All 3 Zamboanga branches with contact numbers
- Cultural heritage messaging (Kahawa Sug, Bangbang Sug)
- Social media integration

**Why it works:**
> "Dennis Coffee Garden has been around since 1962. Their website tells that story in a way Instagram posts never could. When you land on their site, you immediately understand: this isn't just another café, this is heritage. That story is what makes people choose them over random coffee shops."

**The pitch angle:**
> "Dennis Coffee Garden uses their website to tell their story - 60+ years of Sulu coffee tradition. Your business has a story too. A website lets you control that narrative. On Facebook, you're just another post in the feed. On your own website, you're the main character."

### How to Use These Case Studies

**When pitching cafés/restaurants:**
> "Have you seen Dennis Coffee Garden's website? They're local, been around since the 60s. Their site shows their full menu, all their branches, and their whole story. When someone searches 'coffee Zamboanga', they show up. That's what a website does that Facebook can't."

**When pitching any business that wants to grow:**
> "Look at Taza Coffee. They started here in Zamboanga, but now they ship nationwide because their website has a full online store. A website isn't just a digital brochure - it's a growth tool."

**When they say "but I'm just a small business":**
> "Dennis Coffee Garden is a small business too. Three branches, local focus. But their website makes them look established and professional. When tourists search for 'where to eat in Zamboanga', businesses with websites rank higher than Facebook pages. Size doesn't matter - online presence does."

## The Golden Rules

1. **Lead with value, not price** - Show the demo before discussing cost
2. **Be genuinely interested** - Fake enthusiasm is obvious
3. **Make ROI concrete** - Use their actual numbers
4. **Remove all pressure** - Desperate salespeople lose deals
5. **Follow up, but don't stalk** - Persistence ≠ annoyance
6. **Let the work speak** - A great demo closes more than any pitch
